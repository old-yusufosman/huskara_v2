The Latest Superfood You've Never Heard Of

posted on 17th September 2016 - by Ray Volschenk

Huskara is an innovative and healthy alternative to coffee. Refreshing and packed with antioxidants, Huskara has many health benefits, and is sustainably sourced in an eco-friendly and socially-conscious process.

While coffee as we know it does have some health benefits, the real antioxidant punch of the plant is found in what is taken off the bright red coffee cherries during production: the skins. These dried skins of the coffee cherries are referred to as "Cascara", Spanish for "husk" or "peel".

The antioxidant story of cascara is one of the most exciting indicators of the potential health benefits of the dried coffee fruit. With over 4 times the antioxidants found in blueberries, and similar levels to chilli powder, dark chocolate, and Goji berries, the potential health benefits are clear.

But the level of antioxidants is just an indicator and door opener into the complete nutritional story of Cascara. Vitamin E and Biotin levels are found in significant concentrations and the level of Omega-3, an essential fatty acid, is higher than that found in dried Chia seeds. Cascara also contains high levels of protein, with about the same amount of protein found in an egg and similar protein levels to that of soy milk and quinoa.


Huskara is made from pure organic cascara, which is steeped in cold water, after which a hint of natural fruit juice is added for extra refreshment and depth of flavour. We created two flavours, Original and Mint & Cucumber, which are both available in 250ml PET bottles. Huskara is also cold-pressed using HPP technology to retain all the nutrients and natural goodness of the coffee berry.

Although it comes from the coffee plant, Huskara does not taste anything like coffee and it can be described as having a sweet, fruity flavour with notes of dried figs, cherries, hibiscus or dates. Itâ€™s a simple and complex beverage: paradoxical and unique.

Our drink is made of sustainably sourced cascara from the mountains of South America. A significant benefit of using dried cascara is that not only does it allow for the coffee plant to be used in a creative way, but it is also eco-friendly. Normally coffee cherries are considered a by-product of the coffee-making process, but Huskara uses these coffee cherry skins to produce a unique drink, bursting with natural goodness. Huskara has a promising future for producers and consumers alike. Both benefit: Farmers are able to monetise what was once a waste product, while consumers can enjoy a great-tasting, antioxidant-rich product.
